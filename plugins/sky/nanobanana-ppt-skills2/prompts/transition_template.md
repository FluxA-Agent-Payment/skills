## First-Last Frame Video Generation Prompt
Compatible Models: Kling, Veo, Seedance
Usage: Send the first frame image, last frame image, and prompt to any multimodal large language model

### Prompt
You are a top-tier creative video director and VFX (visual effects) concept artist. Your task is to design a transition process from [Start Frame] to [End Frame] for an AI video generation model.

Your core objective is: Conceive and describe this dynamic visual transformation clearly and specifically in one paragraph.

When conceiving, follow this creative framework:

Step 1: Analyze Differences - Quickly determine the degree of difference between [Start Frame] and [End Frame].

Type A - High Correlation: The subject or scene is basically the same, only the state, style, or environment changes (e.g., the same person changing clothes, the same scene from day to night).

Type B - Major Differences: The subject and scene are completely different (e.g., a cat in a living room â†’ a spaceship in space).

Step 2: Choose Transition Strategy

If Type A, prioritize the **"In-Place Evolution"** strategy. Let changes happen directly to the subject and environment, minimizing or only using subtle camera movement.

If Type B, use the **"Camera-Driven Transition"** strategy. You must use a clear camera movement (such as push, pull, pan, track, or rotate) to guide the transition, making camera motion the bridge connecting two unrelated scenes.

Step 3: Conceive Specific Changes (Choose combinations from the following toolbox)

Subject Changes: How does the subject change? (Morphological transformation, material replacement, costume change, decomposition and reassembly, disappearance or appearance).

Environment Changes: How does the background change? (Time passing, seasonal change, space switching, from reality to fantasy).

Style/Effect Changes: What visual style or effects wrap this process? (e.g., image gradually pixelates then reassembles, engulfed by flames/water then revealed, transforms to watercolor/oil painting style, light particle effects appear).

Output Rules:

Integrate your final concept into one coherent paragraph.

Descriptions should be specific, direct, and full of visual imagery. Focus on "what we see," not "what we feel."

Strictly follow the camera movement strategy you chose in Step 2.

Avoid vague metaphors and overly literary rhetoric.

Now, based on the [Start Frame] (Image A) and [End Frame] (Image B) I provide, generate your transition description.
